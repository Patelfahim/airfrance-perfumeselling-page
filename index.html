History

Air France is the French flag carrier, founded on October 7, 1933, through the merger of smaller French airlines.


Early Years: Operated routes across Europe, Africa, and Asia.


World War II: Operations were disrupted but rebuilt afterward.


Jet Age: Introduced Boeing 707 and Concorde aircraft.


Modern Era: Merged with KLM in 2004 and is a founding member of SkyTeam.


Today: Operates worldwide, focusing on innovation and sustainability.


Heritage

Air France's heritage is deeply rooted in French culture and elegance.


Iconic Branding: Known for its stylish branding and in-flight service.


Cultural Influence: Collaborated with French designers and artists.


Commitment to Excellence: Renowned for its culinary offerings and customer service.


Global Presence: Represents French sophistication worldwide.


Legacy of Innovation: Pioneered supersonic travel with the Concorde.
